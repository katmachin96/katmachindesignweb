Background

Nomad Rides is a Y-Combinator backed commission-free ridesharing service. While traditional ridesharing services take 25-50% of fares as commissions, Nomad allows its drivers to keep the entirety of their fares for a small monthly fee of $25. Drivers earn more while riders pay less.



Prompt

Ride-sharing service that pays 100% of fares to drivers! Nomad Rides centers around creating more economic mobility for its drivers.



Challenge

How can Nomad rides become a competitive and unique force in the ridesharing industry?



Goals


1) Increase user Trust & safety

2) Increase driver compensation

3) Optimize ride selection

    Usability Testing


    Hypotheses

    • We believe creating an account and ordering a ride will take less than a minute and 30 seconds
    • Users will generally find that ordering a ride is easy and straightforward, assuming that all or most of the users have ordered an Uber or Lyft before. Users will find a friends feed by clicking on the lower bar of the screen.

    Test Instructions

    Introduction
    Imagine you are a college student and you need to book a ride to a destination.
    Tasks
    1) Sign up and create an account [Success: Yes, No]
    2) Order a standard ride and complete a ride [Success: Yes, No]
    3) View the friends feed [Success: Yes, No]
    4) Order another ride and cancel before driver arrives [Success: Yes, No]
    Questions
    What frustrates you most about the app?
    If you had a magic wand, how would you improve the app? What did you like about the application?
    How likely are you to recommend this site to a friend or colleague (0=Not at all likely, and 10=Very Likely)?

    Reflections

    What I learned

    • Work on the privacy section of the interface experience. Allow users to choose to share on the friends feed
    • Researching the depths of Google’s Material Design guidelines to validate user accessibility with ideating visual design components.

    Challenges

    • Having the ease of a safe ride with convenience
    • Clearer distinction between the current location and the arrival
    • smoother implementation of scheduled rides

    Next Steps

    • Conduct A/B testing with the first prototype using Principle or Invision.

    • Increase color contrast ratio for text, icons for better accessibility
